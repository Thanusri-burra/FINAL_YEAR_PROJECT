CampusGuard

A Multi-Modal AI-Based Campus Safety System
Integrating Video Analytics, Speech Recognition & NLP

📘 Project Abstract

CampusGuard is a multi-modal AI-based campus safety framework designed to overcome the limitations of traditional manual surveillance systems. By integrating video, audio, and text analytics, the system enables proactive threat detection, faster response times, and intelligent decision-making across campus environments.

🎯 Motivation

Growing campus populations and open-access environments make manual CCTV monitoring inefficient. Human-based surveillance suffers from fatigue, delayed response, and missed incidents.

💡 Our Solution

CampusGuard automates surveillance using AI models capable of understanding visual, audio, and textual cues to identify threats proactively rather than reactively.

🎥 Violence Detection

Uses a CNN-LSTM architecture where CNN extracts spatial features from video frames and LSTM models temporal motion to detect violent behavior accurately.

🗣 Abusive Language Detection

Surveillance audio is converted to text using ASR, followed by CNN-based NLP classifiers that identify abusive or threatening speech with high accuracy.

🚨 Emergency Keyword Detection

A lightweight TF-IDF + Naive Bayes model detects emergency words like “help” or “danger” to generate rapid alerts with minimal computation cost.

🎒 Lost & Found AI

Uses pretrained ResNet embeddings and cosine similarity to match lost item images with CCTV frames, returning camera ID, location, timestamp, and confidence score.

🏆 Key Contributions

  • Multi-modal AI-based campus safety framework
  • CNN-LSTM violence detection from CCTV
  • Audio-based abusive speech recognition
  • Efficient emergency keyword detection
  • Object re-identification using visual similarity

🔮 Conclusion & Future Scope

CampusGuard demonstrates how integrated AI models significantly enhance campus security by reducing false alarms and improving situational awareness. Future enhancements include real-time deployment, transformer-based models, edge optimization, and mobile alert integration.
